About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at NCKU specializing in crustal deformation, interseismic fault slip modeling, and seismic hazard assessment. My research focuses on tectonically active zones such as the Himalayas, Taiwan, and the Philippines. I integrate GNSS, InSAR, and advanced Bayesian and AI/ML techniques to constrain fault kinematics and understand long-term earthquake behavior.

Research Interests

Geodesy, Earthquake Physics, Fault Mechanics, Interseismic Modeling, Machine Learning in Geosciences, Probabilistic Inversion, Active Tectonics
